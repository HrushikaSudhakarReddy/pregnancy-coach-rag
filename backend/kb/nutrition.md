# Nutrition Basics (General Info)
- Include a variety of foods: vegetables, fruits, whole grains, protein sources (beans, lentils, eggs, poultry, tofu, fish low in mercury), and healthy fats.
- Hydration is important; water needs differ by person and environment.
- **Food safety** matters: avoid unpasteurized products, undercooked meats/eggs, and high-mercury fish.
- Manage aversions and nausea with small, frequent meals and bland options if needed.
- Cultural and personal dietary patterns can be accommodated while ensuring diverse nutrient intake.
