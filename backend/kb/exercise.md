# Exercise in Pregnancy (General Info)
- For many people with uncomplicated pregnancies, moderate-intensity activity is encouraged if approved by their provider.
- Aim for around **150 minutes per week** of moderate activity spread across the week.
- Examples: walking, stationary cycling, swimming, low-impact aerobics, pregnancy-adapted strength training.
- Keep intensity at a **conversational pace** (approx RPE 4–6/10).
- Avoid activities with risk of abdominal trauma or high fall risk.
- After about **20 weeks**, modify exercises that require lying flat on the back; consider side-lying or seated alternatives.
- Warm-up and cool-down are important; include gentle mobility.
- Stop exercise and seek care if you experience warning signs like chest pain, severe headache/vision changes, vaginal bleeding, or decreased fetal movement.
